Primary Muscle Group Exercises

Today we are going to look at some of the best excercises for the chest, back, shoulders, and legs, three of the main muscle groups asthetics-wise and for many applications in lifting. These are three of the bigger muscle groups and support many of the smaller muscles as they function. Shoulders particularly support even some of the other main muscle groups so it is important to lift correctly in order to use the targeted muscles correctly and promote shoulder health.

Below are a several of many excercises that are the best for each group for muscle growth. Of course depending on what your goals are your training routine may look different than others, but no matter what style you choose the way you lift is very important. Slower, more controlled motions are always better than jerking the weight, you may not be abe to lift as much weight but progress will come faster. Just remember that progress may not be seen over weeks or maybe months, this is the long game, persistance, good training, good diet, and plenty of sleep will assure change over a long enough time frame, so keep vigilant!

Before You Start

Be sure to warm up properly before going into working sets, lifting cold drastically increases the risk of pulling a muscle or injury. Warming up can be anything from running or biking to dynamicly streching. Static streching is also very popular today, however it is not as effective before a workout and your run the risk of pulling something since the blood flow to the muscles and ligaments has not been increased. The main priority is to get blood flowing throughout the body and warm up the muscles. You may then start to work up in weight, four or five jumps, until you are at your main working sets.
